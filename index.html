Attractions

Trinity College

Home to the Book of Kells
Created in 800 AD

Trinity College has been the educating alma mater of my family and is, the oldest College in Dublin. It is also houses one of Ireland's oldest book, The Book of Kells. This is a must see and located in the heart of Dublin. The city's hustle and bustle melts away as soon as you step inside the college's walls!

  • Founded in 1592 by Queen Elizabeth
  • Ranked best in Ireland
  • Notable alumni include, Samuel Beckett & Oscar Wilde
Book of Kells
A Must see

National Gallery

Gailearaí Náisiúnta na hÉireann
Home to some the best Irish & European art

Located beside Leinster house and Clare street. It has an extensive collection of Irish, Italian and Dutch paintings

  • Founded in 1854 but the doors did not open until 1864
  • The Current director is Sean Rainbird
National Gallery
For Art goers

Guinness Storehouse

Home of the Black stuff
9,000 Year lease

The best-known drink in the world, you will not find a better pint than the Guinness Storehouse. It has seven floors, the Gravity Bar located at the top has views of the city to go with the best pint of Guinness you have ever tasted. It has received over 4 million visitors since its opening in 2000. You cannot vist Dublin with-out coming here.

  • Founded by Arthur Guinness
  • Price of admission 20 euros
Guinness Storehouse
Enjoy your pint

Famine Memorial

1,000,000 Died
1,000,000 Emigrated

The Famine is part of Ireland's history. It was caused by a potato disease and compounded by negligent British landlords. A third of the population was depended on the potato and it caused the fall of the Irish population by 25%, With the current global climate I felt this must be added and our thoughts go out to today's refugee crisis.

  • Sailed from Custom House Quay
  • Crafted by Dublin sculptor Rowan Gillespie
  • Observe, remember and learn
Please no selfies

Sandycove

Cuas an Ghainimh
Tiny Beach

Sandycove is a very small beach but not lacking in character. The location is featured in the opening of James Joyce's Ulysses. It is worth taking a break from the city and getting on the dart out to Dun Laoghaire. Walk down to beach popping into Teddy's Ice Cream along the way and enjoying the fresh sea air. If you are hungry, pop into the Fish Shack Cafe where on of best fish in Dublin can be found.

  • Click the seaside button next to the map see its location
  • The dart is the best way to get out there
The Dart
Enjoy the sea air.
