Trinity College

Home to the book of Kells
Created in 800 AD

Trinity College has been the educating home of my family, the oldest College in Dublin and home to Ireland's oldest book, The book of Kells. This is a must see and locationed in the heart of Dublin, the city desapears in the heart of its walls.

  • Founded in 1592 by Queen Elizabeth 1
  • Ranked best in Ireland
  • Notable people Samuel Beckett & Oscar Wilde
Book of Kells
A Must see

National Gallery

Gailearaí Náisiúnta na hÉireann
Home of some the best Irish & European art

Located beside Leinster house and Clare street. It has an extensive collection of Irish, Italian and Dutch paintings

  • Founded in 1854 but the doors did not open until 1864
  • Current director is Sean Rainbird
National Gallery

Guinness Storehouse

Home of the Black stuff
9,000 Year lease

The best known drink in the world you will not find a better pint than the Guinness Storehouse. Seven floors with the best view of the city to go with the best pint of Guinness you have ever tasted in the Gravity Bar. Recevied over 4 million vistors since its opening in 2000 you cannot come to Dublin with out coming here.

  • Founded by Arthur Guinness
  • Price of admission 20 euros

Famine Memorial

1,000,000 Died
1,000,000 Emigrated

The Famine is part of Ireland's history caused by a potato diesase and componded by neglaten British landlords. A third of the population was depended on the potato and it caused the fall of the Irish population by 25% With the current global climate I felt this must be added and thought goes out to today's refugee crisis.

  • Sailed from Custom House Quay
  • Crafted by Dublin sculptor Rowan Gillespie

Sandycove

Cuas an Ghainimh
Tiny Beach

Sandycove is a very small beach but not lacking in charter. The locale is featured in the opening of James Joyce Ulysses. It is worth taking a break from the city and getting on the dart out to Dun Laoghaire. Walk down to beach popping into Teddys Ice Cream along the way and enjoying the fresh sea air. If you are hungery pop into the Fish Shake Cafe where the best fish in Dublin can be had.

  • Click the sea side button below the map
  • Dart is the best way to get out there
